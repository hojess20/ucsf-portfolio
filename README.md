#### [Survival Differences Across PAM50 Subtypes in Breast Cancer](https://github.com/hojess20/ucsf-portfolio/tree/main/SurvivalAnalysis)
![Kaplan Meier Curve](images/kaplan-meier.png)

This project investigates whether PAM50 molecular subtypes are significant prognostic indicators of overall survival in breast cancer, independent of other clinical features like age, tumor size, and histologic grade. Utilizing the METABRIC dataset of nearly 2,000 primary breast tumors, I implemented a multivariable Cox proportional hazards model. The analysis found that the impact of PAM50 molecular subtypes on breast cancer survival is time-dependent. For example, Basal-like, HER2-enriched, and Claudin-low tumors are associated with elevated early mortality risk, while Luminal B tumors show increased hazard later on. Overall, these hazard profiles highlight the importance of flexible modeling approaches for understanding time-varying survival risks in breast cancer.
